How to use the Liquidity Scanner

This panel shows when you open the tool, and hides once you analyze a coin.

🔍

1 · Search any coin

Type a coin name or symbol (e.g. BTC, bitcoin) or tap a quick chip. Pick a timeframe (15min–8H) to control how indicators are calculated, then press Analyze Now. The results below replace this guide.

💵

2 · Price & market data

Current PriceLive price in USD with 24h change %. Green = up, red = down.
24h High / LowThe highest and lowest price in the selected timeframe.
ATH / ATLAll-time high and all-time low — the extreme prices ever reached.
7D / 30D ChangePercent change over the last week and month.
Market CapTotal value = price × circulating supply. Bigger = more established.
24h VolumeHow much was traded in 24h. High volume = active interest.
RankPosition by market cap (#1 = largest).
Circulating / Total SupplyCoins in the market vs. total that will ever exist.
🚦

3 · Liquidity signals

Liquidity StatusIs money flowing in or out? Inflow with strong buying = healthy.
Buy SignalCombines dip + volume + pressure. "Strong Buy" = several positives align.
Sell SignalFlags overbought conditions — when taking profit may be wise.
⏰

4 · Entry & exit timing

Best Time to BuySuggests the better entry moment (now, on a dip, or at support).
Best Time to SellSuggests when to consider exiting (now, at resistance, or at target).
💰

5 · Liquidity flow

Inflow (24h)Estimated buying volume entering the coin.
Outflow (24h)Estimated selling volume leaving the coin.
Net FlowInflow − outflow. Positive (green) = net buying, negative (red) = net selling.
Vol / MCapVolume ÷ market cap. Higher % = more liquid & active.
Buy Pressure0–100% gauge of where price sits in its range. High = buyers in control.
Last Major FlowTime of the last big liquidity movement.
🧠

6 · Fear & Greed index

A market-wide mood gauge from 0–100. Below 25 = extreme fear (others panic-selling — often opportunity). Above 75 = extreme greed (market euphoric — often risky). The needle shows today's reading.

🎯

7 · Key price levels

Support 1 & 2Price floors where buyers tend to step in. Price often bounces here.
Resistance 1 & 2Price ceilings where sellers tend to appear. Price often stalls here.

Example: if price is near Support 1 with a buy signal, it may be a good entry. Near Resistance with a sell signal → consider taking profit.

📈

8 · Technical indicators

RSI (14)Momentum 0–100. Below 30 = oversold (possible buy), above 70 = overbought (possible sell). e.g. RSI 25 → bounce likely.
MACD HistogramTrend strength. Positive = bullish momentum, negative = bearish.
Bollinger %BWhere price sits in its band. Near 0% = cheap edge, near 100% = expensive edge.
Stochastic K/DOverbought/oversold gauge. Below 20 = oversold, above 80 = overbought.
EMA (50)Trend line. Price above = uptrend, below = downtrend.
ATR VolatilityHow much the coin typically moves (%). Higher = riskier swings.
Overall BiasCombines all six into one read: Bullish, Bearish, or Mixed, with a buy/sell bar.
🌊

9 · Deep liquidity

Est. SpreadGap between buy & sell price. Smaller % = easier, cheaper trades.
Depth (±1%)How much money sits within 1% of price. Higher = big orders move price less.
Slippage $10K / $100K / $1MExpected price impact for orders of that size. Lower % = better.
Liquidity GradeA = excellent (easy execution), B = good, C = caution on big orders, D = poor (high slippage).
